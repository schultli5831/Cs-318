The Nine Worlds & Yggdrasil

Völuspá

Stanzas 1-5

Hearing I ask | from the holy races, From Heimdall's sons, | both high and low; Thou wilt, Valfather, | that well I relate Old tales I remember | of men long ago.

I remember yet | the giants of yore, Who gave me bread | in the days gone by; Nine worlds I knew, | the nine in the tree With mighty roots | beneath the mold.

Of old was the age | when Ymir lived; Sea nor cool waves | nor sand there were; Earth had not been, | nor heaven above, But a yawning gap, | and grass nowhere.

Then Bur's sons lifted | the level land, Mithgarth the mighty | there they made; The sun from the south | warmed the stones of earth, And green was the ground | with growing leeks.

The sun, the sister | of the moon, from the south Her right hand cast | over heaven's rim; No knowledge she had | where her home should be, The moon knew not | what might was his, The stars knew not | where their stations were.

Yggdrasil

Yggdrasil is a tree central to the Norse concept of the cosmos. The tree's branches extend into various realms, and various creatures dwell on and around it. The gods go to Yggdrasil daily to assemble at their things, traditional governing assemblies. The branches of Yggdrasil extend far into the heavens, and the tree is supported by three roots that extend far away into other locations; one to the well Uröarbrunnr in the heavens, one to the spring Hvergelmir, and another to the well Mö­misbrunnr. Creatures live within Yggdrasil, including the dragon Nidhöggr, an unnamed eagle, and the stags Dá¡inn, Dvalinn, Duneyrr and Duraprór.

Asgard

In Norse mythology, Asgard (Old Norse ásgardr) is one of the Nine Realms and home to the ádir, tribe of gods. It is surrounded by an incomplete wall attributed to a Hrimthurs riding the stallion Svaöilfari, according to Gylfaginning. Odin and his wife, Frigg, are the rulers of Asgard.

Vanaheimr

Vanaheimr is one of the Nine Realms and the homeland of the Vanir gods, who are associated with fertility, prosperity, and nature. The Vanir were once at war with the Aesir but eventually made peace, and a truce was established between the two tribes of gods.

Alfheim

Alfheim, also known as Álfheimr, is the realm of the Light Elves (Ljósálfar), who are depicted as benevolent beings of light. This realm is often associated with beauty, light, and nature, and is sometimes considered the home of Freyja and Freyr.

Midgard

Midgard is the realm of humans, situated in the center of the Norse cosmos. It is connected to Asgard by the Bifrost bridge and is surrounded by a great ocean. The term 'Midgard' translates to 'middle earth', reflecting its position among the other realms.

Jötunheimr

Jötunheimr is the land of the giants (Jötnar), often seen as the enemies of the gods. This realm is characterized by rugged landscapes, mountains, and deep forests. It embodies chaos and untamed nature, contrasting sharply with the order of Asgard.

Muspelheim

Muspelheim is the fiery realm of fire giants and is often associated with chaos and destruction. It is ruled by the fire giant Surtr, who is prophesied to play a key role in the events of Ragnarök, the end of the world in Norse mythology.

Svartálfar

Svartálfar, or Dark Elves, are creatures associated with the underground and are often considered skilled craftsmen and smiths. They are said to dwell in Svartálfheimr, a realm where they forge weapons and other treasures for the gods.

Niflheim

Niflheim is a cold and misty realm, often associated with ice, fog, and darkness. It is one of the primordial realms, existing before the creation of the world. Niflheim is also linked to Hel, the realm of the dead, ruled by the goddess Hel.

Nidavellir

Nidavellir is the home of the dwarves, known for their exceptional skill in crafting and metalwork. This realm is often depicted as an underground labyrinth filled with forges, where the dwarves create powerful artifacts, including Thor's hammer, Mjölnir.
